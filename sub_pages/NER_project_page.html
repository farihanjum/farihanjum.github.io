Overview of the research problem

Named Entity Recognition (NER) identifies and categorizes entities like people, places, and organizations in text. In low-resource languages, NER is crucial to preserve linguistic diversity by digitizing and structuring local knowledge. It supports domain-specific applications, and ensures culturally relevant data extraction. This inclusion boosts multilingual AI systems,increasing the possibilities of diverse technology solutions. Bangla is considered ambiguous due to its rich morphology, complex grammar, and contextual word usage. Words often have multiple meanings, and their interpretation relies heavily on context, making tasks like word sense disambiguation challenging. The absence of standard orthography for transliterated Bangla text further adds complexity, as spelling variations lead to inconsistencies in NLP applications. Moreover, Bangla has many homophones, and pronoun reference resolution can be challenging due to less explicit subject-verb alignment. These factors make processing Bangla text with precision difficult, posing unique challenges for NLP tasks like Named Entity Recognition and sentiment analysis. These research gaps motivated us to develop a NER system for Bangla.
