SOMAJGYAAN: A Dataset for Evaluating LLMs on Bangla Culture, Social Knowledge, and Low-Resource Language Adaptation

Fariha Anjum Shifa, Muhtasim Ibteda Shochcho, Abdullah Ibne Hanif Arean, Mohammad Ashfaq Ur Rahman, AKM Moshiur Rahman, Ahaj Mahhin Faiak, Md Fahim, M Ashraful Amin, Amin Ahsan Ali, AKM Mahbubur Rahman
Findings of IJCNLP–AACL 2025
Preprint Dataset

Despite significant progress in large language models (LLMs), their knowledge and evaluation continue to be centered around high resource languages, leaving critical gaps in low-resource settings. This raises questions about how effectively LLMs handle subjects that require locally relevant knowledge. To address this challenge, we need a robust dataset that reflects the knowledge of underrepresented regions such as Bangladesh. In this paper, we present SOMAJGYAAN, a Bangla multiple-choice dataset consisting of 4,234 questions, annotated across five levels of difficulty. The questions are drawn from Bangladesh’s National Curriculum and Global Studies textbooks. Our dataset is available at https://github.com/farihanjum/SOMAJGYAAN.

NLP-DU at SemEval-2025 Task 11: Analyzing Multi-label Emotion Detection

Sakib, S., Faiak, A., Arean, A. I. H., Fariha Anjum Shifa
SemEval-2025 (ACL), Vienna, pp. 1269–1275
PDF

This paper describes NLP-DU’s entry to SemEval-2025 Task 11 on multi-label emotion detection. We investigated the efficacy of transformer-based models and propose an ensemble approach that combines multiple models. Our experiments demonstrate that the ensemble outperforms individual models under the dataset constraints, yielding superior performance on key evaluation metrics.

Under Review

SimPrompt: Few-Shot Bangla NER with Similarity-Driven Demonstration Retrieval

Fariha Anjum Shifa, Sarker Tanveer Ahmed Rumee, Moinul Islam Zaber
Under Review at EACL
Preprint

Named Entity Recognition (NER) is a key task in the field of natural language processing, but it comes with its own set of challenges, especially for low-resource languages like Bangla. The main hurdles stem from the lack of annotated data and the intricate features of the language. To tackle this issue, we present SimPrompt, an innovative few-shot prompting framework designed to boost Bangla NER. It does this by utilizing sentence-level semantic similarity to dynamically retrieve demonstrations. Our method involves gathering a collection of annotated examples and using a fine-tuned Sentence-Transformer model to pick the k most relevant demonstrations for each query. These selected examples then help prompt a large language model for accurate entity classification. When we tested SimPrompt on the Bangla subsets of the MultiCoNER and BanNERD benchmarks, it consistently outperformed static, random, and sparse (TF-IDF) retrieval methods. In 3-shot settings, our approach achieved an impressive accuracy of 0.81 and an F1-score of 0.58 on MultiCoNER, while on BanNERD, it reached an accuracy of 0.92 and an F1-score of 0.76. Our thorough analysis reveals that this similarity-driven prompting not only reduces entity misclassification but also significantly enhances the recognition of rare categories, leading to strong performance improvements as the number of demonstrations increases. This work highlights the effectiveness of entity-aware demonstration selection, paving the way for better NER in Bangla and other underrepresented languages.

MET-BNER: A Dataset for Metonymy Resolution in Bangla Named Entity Recognition

Fariha Anjum Shifa et al.
In Preparation
Project Page

This work introduces MET-BNER, a novel dataset designed to address the challenge of metonymy resolution in Bangla Named Entity Recognition. Metonymy, where one entity is referred to by another related entity, poses unique challenges in NLP tasks, especially in morphologically rich languages like Bangla.

A Novel Approach to Classifying Plastic-Degrading Enzymes using Convolutional Neural Networks

Fariha Anjum Shifa*, Soharth Hasnat*, Shabab Murshed Shoummo, Sarker Tanvir Ahmed Rumee, MST Murshida Mahbub
Poster accepted, 23rd Science Council of Asia Conference
Preprint Poster Project Page

The growing accumulation of plastic waste presents a significant environmental challenge, necessitating innovative approaches to mitigate its impact. Enzymatic degradation has emerged as a promising solution. This study introduces a novel feature extraction strategy for identifying plastic-degrading enzymes. Among seven supervised machine learning models, the CNN model demonstrated the best performance, achieving an accuracy of 0.96.

A Risk-score Based Model for Dynamics of Infectious Disease Spread

Fariha Anjum Shifa, Humayra Jahan, Upama Kabir
bioRxiv preprint, 2024
PDF Poster Project Page

Infectious viral diseases that are newly developing or returning have threatened humanity throughout history. This research exercises the exposure level of different nearby areas of a specific region to get insight into the progression of infectious diseases. We introduced a method to compute risk score considering the risk probability to each node in an area.
